Ingredients | Serves 2 | 15 minutes
One package baby spinach or Kale (8oz - Coarsley chopped)
1 can chickpeas or 1 1/2 cups cooked chickpeas
1/4 tsp paprika
1 onion rough chopped
6oz or 1 cup mushrooms chopped
2-3 tblsp olive oil or avocado oil
1/2 tsp garlic powder/crushed garlic
2 tbsp roasted sunflower seeds/pine nuts (optional topping)
2 tbsp water
sea salt and pepper to taste

Instructions
In a large saute pan , on medium heat, cook the onions in the olive oil (optional - add tumeric)
When they are browned, add in the mushrooms and cook until the mushrooms soften ~ 3 minutes.
Season with sea salt and garlic (powder), paprika (optional - more spices)
Stir and cook for 1 minute
Stir in the chickpeas till all ingredients are well combined. Let the chickpeas heat up for 2 minutes, stirring as they warm
Pour 2 tbsp water into the bean and mushroom mixture and bring to a boil. Meanwhile, place the fresh spinach or baby kale over the top of the chickpeas
Reduce heat to medium low. Cover the pan and allow spinach to cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until wilted
Stir the spinach into the chickpeas and mushrooms. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Turn heat to medium and let the mixture continue to cook till excess liquid has evaporated
Sprinkle with roasted sunflower seeds/pine nuts, and serve
